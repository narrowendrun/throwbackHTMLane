Instant Messaging (IM)

Instant messaging is a service where people can send texts and reply to them in real time with the use of an internet connection.
The concept of instant messaging existed since the mid-1960s,
however back then, people used it as notification systems for services like printing. With the advances in technology, instant messaging took a new form during the mid-80s and 90s. A huge population of people started communicating using instant messaging services because of the availability of a steady and secure internet connection. Since the start of the second millennium, many companies have started their own messaging services, some of which are exclusive to their devices only.
Few such instant messaging services which are still widely used by people all around the world, at the time of writing this e-book are, Whatsapp Messenger, Facebook Messenger, Skype, iMessage, Google Hangouts and Yahoo!Messenger.
As of April 2016, there are approximately 900 million active users for Facebook Messenger alone! Nowadays, instant messaging services have improved so much that user can now video call or voice call their friends, send various types of media files and documents, send voice messages, and much more!
